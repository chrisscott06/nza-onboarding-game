# NET ZERO HERO — Story Bible (v1)

*The complete narrative arc. Four worlds, one level each = a finished Climate
Action Plan. Build World 1 now; the rest is locked vision Will & Imi build
against.*

> Filed in the repo as the narrative source of truth. **Name note:** the
> recurring foil is **"Mr Net Stupid Zero"** (not "bloody") — corrected
> throughout from the v1 draft.

Voice: planet's in trouble, we're cheeky about it. British, dry, warm. Funny
about the problem, never bleak. The four-pillar Climate Action Plan is the frame,
worn lightly. The hero's an underdog, not a superhero. **The deep message (the
decodED thesis): a Climate Action Plan is too big for one person — you win by
getting everyone involved and getting real data into the right hands.**

---

## 1. THE HERO

**You play a Student** — keen, frustrated that nobody's doing anything, decides
to start it themselves. Your actual face goes on the character (the face system
is already built).

Why the Student: most relatable for the schools/LinkedIn audience, funniest, most
universal. The "kid who just starts" is a great underdog.

**Character-choice foundation (built now, second path written later):** the game
supports choosing your hero. Path two — **the Sustainability Lead** (a teacher
handed the whole plan on top of their day job) — is the same arc through a
different door: the Lead's barrier is *overwhelm and isolation*, the Student's is
*powerlessness*. Both learn the same lesson. Only the Student path is fully
written now; the Lead path is an easy variation later (a good Will/Imi task).

**The arc of the hero (across all four worlds):** you start **alone and
underpowered** — the world's dirty, you've got a four-part plan you don't
understand, and no help. Each world you clear, you **pick up an ally** who carries
one piece of the puzzle and one chunk of real data. By the end you're not a lone
hero — you're the one who *rallied everyone and got the numbers into the right
hands.* That's the win. That's decodED.

---

## 2. THE RECURRING CAST

**The data motif (the spine of the whole thing):** you're weak when you're
*guessing*, strong when you have *real data*. Power-ups are knowledge. The hero
who knows their numbers wins. This is decodED, made playable — never stated as an
advert, just felt.

**Per world: one GOODIE who joins you + one FOIL who heckles + a BOSS at the end.**

- **Goodies (allies you gain, one per world)** — each is a group a real plan
  needs: the grid champion, the resilience engineer, the nature ranger, the next
  generation. Gaining them = the plan needs everyone.
- **The recurring foil — Mr Net Stupid Zero** — not evil, just *inertia*: "too
  hard, too expensive, won't work." He's every excuse a real sustainability lead
  hears. He pops up in each world to heckle; beating the world beats the excuse.
  (Caricature of a *stance*, never a real person.)
- **Bosses** — the big fossil-era villain of each pillar.

**The big bad: THE OIL BARON** — top-hatted fossil tycoon, the final villain
behind it all. He's the World 1 boss *and* the recurring hand behind every world,
defeated for good in World 4.

Action Plan all along, and the way you won — everyone involved, real data in hand
— is exactly how it's done for real.* (Soft decodED reveal here, earned, at the
very end.)

## 4. WORLD 1 — "POWER UP THE GRID" (BUILD THIS)

### 4.1 The theme — broader than just the grid

Decarbonisation isn't only electricity. World 1 covers the whole carbon picture,
which makes the level richer:
- **Energy** — solar, wind, battery storage (the core, already designed).
- **Transport** — ICE cars are hazards; **hop into an EV** as a ride/power-up
  (the funny bit — you commandeer an electric car and zoom through a section).
- **Supply chain / aviation** — background hazards: a plane trailing emissions,
  delivery trucks. Dodge-and-learn flavour.
- **Waste/recycling** — optional collectible: bins you sort for bonus points.

Signature mechanic stays the **storage meter** (renewables only bank if you have
storage; full = curtailment; spend it on a grid-surge dash).

### 4.2 The level shape (one level, the Celeste/Mario teaching curve)

1. **Open dim & dirty** — gas haze, grid running on fossil. Tutorial-safe: learn
   to move, grab first solar.
2. **Meet your first ally** (narrative beat — see 4.3). The world brightens a touch.
3. **Storage section** — learn batteries; the storage-meter mechanic taught safe,
   then challenged.
4. **Transport stretch** — ICE cars as hazards; the **EV ride** gag — hop in, zoom
   a fast section.
5. **The heckler** — Mr Net Stupid Zero appears (narrative beat), throws boilers,
   you push past.
6. **Supply-chain gauntlet** — planes/trucks overhead, the grid-surge dash through
   a dark stretch.
7. **The Oil Baron boss** (narrative beat + fight) — defeat by flipping the grid
   fully green.
8. **Win** — world snaps clean-blue, Minister Milirenew salutes, Pillar One done,
   Baron scarpers.

### 4.3 The narrative beats (the "stop and meet them" moments)

These are **dialogue beats** — action pauses, a character appears, lines play
(cheeky voice), control returns. Built as a reusable cutscene system (works for
every world; Will & Imi can use it).

**BEAT 1 — Opening (your bedroom/school gate):**
> YOU: Nobody's doing anything about the climate. Fine. I'll start.
> *(beat)*
> YOU: ...I have no idea what I'm doing. There's a four-part plan. Step one: the grid's filthy. Let's fix that.

**BEAT 2 — Meet MINISTER MILIRENEW (goodie, joins you):**
A beaming minister in hi-vis-over-a-suit jogs in.
> MILIRENEW: Brilliant! A volunteer! I'm pushing clean power but I can't be everywhere. You take the grid — grab every bit of clean energy you can. Here, have some turbines!
> *(gains: policy-boost power-up + first real data — "UK offshore wind: 41% capacity factor. Worth more than solar. That's why it's up high.")*
> YOU: Real numbers. Suddenly I feel less useless.

**BEAT 3 — MR NET STUPID ZERO (foil, heckles):**
Red-faced, loud tie, jabbing finger.
> NET STUPID ZERO: It'll NEVER work! Too expensive! The wind doesn't even blow at night! Give up!
> YOU: That's what storage is for, mate. *(blasts past)*
> *(beating this stretch = beating the excuse)*

**BEAT 4 — THE OIL BARON (boss):**
Top hat, cigar, sat on a giant gas plant.
> OIL BARON: Ah, the little hero. You can't switch ME off. I AM the grid.
> YOU: Not for much longer.
> *(fight: flip the electrify-switches until the plant goes green and powers down)*

**BEAT 5 — Win:**
> OIL BARON: *(fleeing)* This isn't over! There are three more parts to your little plan — and I'll be waiting!
> MILIRENEW: Pillar one: DONE. The grid's clean. But he's right — there's more. Adaptation next. Onwards, hero!
> *(World map: World 2 "Beat the Heat" hints unlocked... coming soon.)*

### 4.4 World 1 cast summary
- **Goodie:** Minister Milirenew (grid champion; gives policy-boost + first data).
- **Foil:** Mr Net Stupid Zero (the excuse).
- **Boss:** The Oil Baron (round 1; scarpers, returns in W4).

## 5. HOW IT SELLS DECODED (never as an advert)

The game makes you *feel* the real problem: starting alone, overwhelmed, not
knowing your numbers — then the relief of gaining allies and real data. The win
is mobilising people + data, which is literally what decodED does. The only
explicit decodED moment is a soft, earned line at the very end of World 4: *"That
was a Climate Action Plan. Want to do the real one? There's a tool for that."*
Everything before is empathy, not sales.
